                            Windows 10 looks near identical to 8 with with an app happy start menu and start screen, since the menu can expand fully. As apps can now be on the desktop, Windows looks like a tablet-based system even more. Retro computer users from xp or before may not enjoy this at all, aside from those only missing the start menu. Windows 8.0 was near identical to 7 if you ignored the app screen. Some features were already notable in 7, that were for use in 8, such as explorer windows being able to 'stick' to the edges of the screen.

                            Many primarily disliked 8 because of the Metro design of multicoloured tiles on a blue background (an aquired taste). The media eventually changed their optimistic initial reviews of 8, in order to stay on the public's side. The trolling sheep soon started kicking online and commenting without even trying the system for long, and for the majority, not at all. The Metro design isn't going anywhere, and is clearly staying with 10. At least the app screen was separate in 8, and not hard to use. Fact - it was actually just as fast with a mouse than a touchscreen using scrollwheel, and not as awkward as a touchpad. Windows 10's control in it's full screen 'app mode' will be no different. If you couldn't shut down an app or shut off your system in 8, then you missed the intro upon first boot, as windows actually told how to do it.

                            Windows 10 is 8.1 with a menu, more custom features and device networking options, and the critics will rave. There's nothing wrong with that if it lives up to it's claims, and 8.1 users will upgrade no doubt, but will be also laughing in the process at those that thumbed down 8 and 8.1, are now using a very similar system. It's success will be all based on hype, so I would bet if 10 was actually named 8.3, it would not be as hyped up as it is now. Still upset? You could always give up on 8.1 and 10 and have a go with Linux, or buy apple, but you can't just hope everything will come to you or work correctly without being shown. Those OS are also very different to windows, and linux and it's much needed terminal might be too much for some, which you will need to often use quite often (the equivalent of DOS commands). I'm afraid with any change, users just have to learn.

                            ps If you want to try Linux, Mint is the closest you will get to a traditional windows environment. If you don't rely on major brand name software or videogames, then it's worth a try. You can boot it up from cd to try without installation.
